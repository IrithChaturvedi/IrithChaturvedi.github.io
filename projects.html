Analyzing the Real Estate Market in the US Northeast:

Motivation

The Northeastern region of the USA includes Pennsylvania, Maine, New York, New Jersey, Massachusetts, Connecticut, Rhode Island, New Hampshire, and Vermont. Despite its historical and cultural significance, affordability remains a challenge for many residents.

Alternative Places in the U.S. Northeast

  • Why Stay in the Northeast? This project highlights economic stability in the Northeast while identifying affordable alternatives.
  • Focus on Affordability: Identifying cities that offer career opportunities without excessive costs.
  • Avoiding Newly Saturated Cities: Instead of moving to popular Southern cities, this project suggests sister cities with strong career prospects.
  • Strategic Move from New York: Providing data-driven insights to help individuals relocate to affordable cities without compromising economic growth.

Methods

Data Retrieval

Four datasets were used for analysis:

  • Per Capita Income per US State (Kaggle)
  • Updated House Prices of Northeastern US States (Kaggle)
  • Population Statistics of US States (Data.gov)
  • Pittsburgh Home Prices Dataset (census.data.gov)

Data Processing

  • Data Cleaning: Removed null values, dropped unwanted columns.
  • Data Sorting: Grouped data by state, filtered population data for 2010-2019 trends.
  • Analysis & Visualization: Used Python Pandas, Folium, and Matplotlib to manipulate data and generate insights.

Machine Learning Model

  • Implemented a Logistic Regression model to predict the best Pittsburgh neighborhood based on budget, size of house, numer of bedrooms, proximity to school and other features.
  • Created a pipeline using StandardScaler() and LogisticRegression() to process and train the data.
  • Applied GridSearchCV with 5-fold cross-validation to optimize hyperparameters.
  • Achieved 95% accuracy in predicting the best US Northeast neighborhood to live in based on your requirements
  • Using Zillow's real estate API, ther results of the model and other features are used to report the 10 best properties that suit the users needs.

Results from Analysis

Population Trends & Cost of Living

  • Southern U.S. states have seen population growth due to lower costs.
  • Cities with a strong job market and moderate living costs are ideal for relocation.
  • Northeastern states, excluding New York, provide stable incomes relative to living expenses.

Recommended Cities

1. Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

  • Average House Price: $160K
  • Diverse Population: 22% Black/African American, 5.5% Asian
  • Average Salary: $64K
  • Why Pittsburgh? Affordable alternative to NYC with economic stability.

2. Hartford, Connecticut

  • Average House Price: $190K
  • Diverse Population: 34% Black/African American, 20% Hispanic
  • Why Hartford? Balance of affordability, diversity, and job opportunities.

Conclusion

This study provides data-driven insights into affordable Northeast cities, helping individuals leaving NYC make informed relocation decisions. Pittsburgh and Hartford emerge as the top recommendations due to their affordability, strong job markets, and economic stability.

Stock Price Performance of Pharmaceutical Companies during COVID-19

Summary

The COVID-19 pandemic significantly impacted global markets, particularly pharmaceutical companies involved in vaccine development. This project examines the correlation between U.S. COVID-19 case and death rates and the stock price movements of Pfizer (PFE), AstraZeneca (AZN), and Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) from 2020 to 2021. The study analyzes market responses to major pandemic events, such as vaccine approvals and peak infection periods.

Research Question

How did fluctuations in U.S. COVID-19 cases and death rates correlate with changes in the stock prices of major pharmaceutical companies during the pandemic?

Data Sources

  • COVID-19 Data: Johns Hopkins University GitHub Repository (Daily case and death data for the U.S.)
  • Stock Price Data: Alpha Vantage API (Weekly adjusted closing prices for selected pharmaceutical stocks)

Approach

The project involved data integration, cleaning, and visualization using Python and Snakemake. Key COVID-19 dates were aligned with stock price movements to identify significant market trends.

Findings

COVID-19 Trends

  • Peak U.S. cases occurred on January 8, 2021, surpassing 300,000 in a single day.
  • January 12, 2021, marked the highest recorded daily death toll, exceeding 4,400 deaths.
  • States with high population densities (e.g., California, New York) had elevated incident rates.

Stock Market Response

  • Stock prices surged following vaccine approvals but exhibited volatility amid uncertainty.
  • Pfizer and AstraZeneca saw stock declines post-vaccine release, possibly due to early skepticism.
  • Johnson & Johnson, with a later vaccine rollout, maintained stronger stock performance into 2021.

Future Work

  • Expand the analysis to include Moderna and Novavax for a broader market assessment.
  • Obtain daily stock price data for a more granular examination of market fluctuations.
  • Explore global stock trends beyond the U.S. for a more comprehensive impact analysis.
