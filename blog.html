Countries Close to My Heart

Vietnam

This Southeast Asian gem has gained prominence among world travelers in the past few years. Within its communist beginnings and colonial history lies a nation epitomizing Asia as a whole. Whether it's the capitals or the rainforests, the longest caves in the world, or the tourist-trodden bays, Vietnam is a budget-friendly and exciting destination to discover—whether you live right across the border or across the seven seas.

Planning Your Trip

1) Visa

The visa process for Vietnam is straightforward, as it offers visa-free entry or e-visa access to most nationalities. Be cautious of third-party websites charging high fees; apply directly through the official Vietnamese government website: Apply for a Vietnam e-Visa. The cost is around USD 25 per person.

2) Duration

The ideal length of stay depends on your budget, vacation days, and itinerary. A recommended duration is 15 days, allowing ample time to explore the country from north to south, beginning in one capital and ending in the other. Vietnam can be visited year-round, though summer months are hot and humid—perfect for enjoying Ha Long Bay.

3) Packing

Pack light for a warm and humid climate. Fun fact: many of your clothes are probably made in Vietnam! Instead of overpacking, consider buying high-quality, affordable clothing locally.

4) Flights

Various airlines operate direct and connecting flights to Vietnam from major international hubs. Look for deals on budget airlines or full-service carriers depending on your comfort preferences.
